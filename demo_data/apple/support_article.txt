If your iPhone isn't charging

Check these steps if your iPhone won't charge or charges more slowly than expected.

Check your cable and adapter

Use the cable and adapter that came with your iPhone, or accessories that are Apple-certified. Damaged or uncertified accessories can prevent charging. Look for visible damage to the cable, especially near the connectors.

Check the charging port

Look at the port on the bottom of your iPhone. Remove any debris with a clean, dry, soft-bristled brush — never use a metal tool. A blocked port is one of the most common reasons iPhones don't charge.

Try a different power source

Some power sources don't provide enough current. Try a different USB adapter, a wall outlet instead of a computer, or a different wall outlet entirely.

Restart your iPhone

A temporary software issue can interrupt charging. Restart your iPhone and connect it to power again.

Update iOS

Outdated software can cause charging issues. Go to Settings > General > Software Update and install any available updates.

Check for damage

If your iPhone has been exposed to liquid or was dropped recently, internal damage may be preventing normal charging. Contact Apple Support or visit an Apple Store.

If none of these steps help

Your iPhone may need service. Contact Apple Support at support.apple.com, use the Apple Support app, or visit an Apple Store or Apple Authorized Service Provider. You can also call 1-800-MY-APPLE.

Don't have an Apple Account? You can still get support as a guest.
